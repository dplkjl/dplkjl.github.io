Peer programming replicates the developer-client relationship during the programming phase. It is also a great teaching opportunity when one developer has more experience than another to share the pool of experience.
Some reasons I think peer programming is rad:

  • Collaboration
  • Teaching
  • Replicating client-developer relationship

During our peer programming session, Karen and Adam used a driver-navigator dynamic. This is where two developers sit side-by-side (or in a line of communication), and only one of them actually writes the code, while the other directs.
